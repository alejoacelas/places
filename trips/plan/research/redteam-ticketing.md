# Red-team: An individual (solo dev) can buy train tickets end-to-end from the terminal by integrating with Trainline — search, pay, and receive the ticket on the phone, all programmatically.

> Intermediate research note — generated by the `derisk-trips-email-slack` workflow on 2026-06-29. This is raw input behind the plan docs, not the final word.

**Verdict: refuted** (confidence: high)

## Realistic path

Split the dream in two. (1) Routing/compare/plan IS doable solo on open data without Google — DB API Marketplace, SNCF/Navitia, National Rail Darwin/OpenLDBWS, transport.rest/Transitous — so build the journey planner and price/plan comparison in the terminal. (2) Purchase is NOT doable end-to-end and unattended: hand off to a sanctioned web/app checkout via a deep-link (ideally as a registered Trainline affiliate through Partnerize), where the user completes payment in Trainline's own PCI-compliant flow and the mobile ticket lands in Trainline's app on their phone (satisfying "carry it on my phone"). Net deliverable: terminal-side "compare + plan + one-tap to the right checkout," not terminal-side card payment + ticket issuance.

## Blockers

- No public/self-serve Trainline API. The only API is Trainline Partner Solutions 'Global API' — explicitly B2B for 'travel sellers/brands', contact-sales only, no developer self-signup, ~12-week average integration. A personal CLI does not qualify; even qualifying means a commercial reseller contract with settlement obligations.
- Trainline ToS Section 12.2 expressly prohibits using 'automated agents, scripts or routine', 'robots, spiders, scripts', scraping/mining data, navigating with non-provided tools, and building 'a similar or competitive site or service'. Scripting the consumer site/app to buy is a direct ToS breach (account ban + legal exposure). Tickets are 'non-transferable' (4.2).
- Payment/PCI: any path where the app itself collects the card and pays the operator puts the solo dev in scope as merchant/processor. PCI DSS responsibility stays with the merchant even when using a third-party processor, plus chargeback/refund/settlement liability — impractical for one person.
- UK regulatory moat (deepest blocker): to actually issue GB National Rail tickets you must be RDG/RSP-accredited — agree a retailing licence with ATOC Commercial & RSP and build or buy an accredited Ticket Issuing System (TIS). Accreditation runs ~£1,000/day, must be re-accredited at least every 3 years, with ongoing connectivity/RSP data charges and possible annual licence fee. Categorically out of reach for a personal project; this accreditation is precisely Trainline's moat.
- Fulfilment: tickets are delivered as e-tickets / mobile barcodes / collection refs bound to the retailer's account and the named passenger and are non-transferable, so a 'terminal-bought' ticket can't be cleanly moved to your phone outside a sanctioned retailer's app/flow.
- No alternative self-serve booking API exists to route around Trainline. Deutsche Bahn 'does not allow direct integrations — you connect via a partner'; DB and SNCF public APIs are routing/real-time only, not booking; aggregators (Distribusion, Turnit, SilverRail) are B2B reseller APIs that still require per-carrier approval plus accurate settlement submission. Same gating everywhere.

## Fallback

Ship "price + plan + deep-link to checkout." Compute and compare journeys (driving vs train vs multimodal) in the terminal from open routing/timetable/fare data, then emit a deep-link into Trainline's (or DB/SNCF's) own checkout with the journey prefilled as far as the public link allows. Register in the Trainline Affiliate Programme via Partnerize (no joining fee, ~3% commission / higher CPA, 30-day cookie, Quicklink deep-links) so the handoff is sanctioned and even monetizable. The user taps to pay inside the operator's PCI-compliant flow and the mobile ticket lives in the operator's app — delivering the practical goal (plan in terminal, buy in one tap, carry on phone) with zero ToS breach, zero PCI scope, and zero accreditation cost. Honest caveat: affiliate deep-links typically prefill a search/journey, not a fully populated cart with passenger+payment, so it is "one-tap to the right checkout," not literal unattended terminal purchase.

## Evidence

- Trainline's only API is the B2B 'Global API' under Trainline Partner Solutions, aimed at travel sellers/brands, ~12-week average integration, contact-sales with no self-serve developer model; eligibility/contracts/payment/settlement are reserved for direct partner discussion.
  — https://tps.thetrainline.com/our-products/global-api/
- Trainline T&Cs Section 12.2 prohibit automated agents/scripts/routines, robots/spiders/scripts, scraping/mining data, navigating with non-provided tools, and building a similar/competitive service; tickets are non-transferable (4.2).
  — https://www.thetrainline.com/terms
- Sanctioned fallback exists: Trainline Affiliate Programme via Partnerize — deep-links/Quicklink, ~3% commission (higher CPA available), 30-day cookie, no joining fee, open to apps/websites.
  — https://www.thetrainline.com/about-us/partnerships/affiliates
- Selling GB National Rail tickets requires RDG/RSP accreditation: a retailing licence agreed with ATOC Commercial & RSP plus an accredited Ticket Issuing System, accreditation ~£1,000/day, re-accreditation at least every 3 years, ongoing service/data charges.
  — https://www.raildeliverygroup.com/our-services/retail-commercial/how-to-become-a-retailer/accreditation.html
- Deutsche Bahn does not allow direct integrations (must connect via a partner); DB and SNCF public APIs (DB API Marketplace, SNCF/Navitia) are routing/real-time focused, not self-serve booking for individuals.
  — https://developers.deutschebahn.com/db-api-marketplace/apis/frontpage
- B2B rail aggregators (e.g. Distribusion) unify carrier content but still require per-carrier approval and accurate sale recording/settlement; merchants retain PCI DSS responsibility even when using third-party processors.
  — https://www.distribusion.com/rail-solutions
- PCI compliance responsibility stays with the merchant even when relying on external payment processors/service providers — confirming the solo dev would carry PCI scope if collecting payment directly.
